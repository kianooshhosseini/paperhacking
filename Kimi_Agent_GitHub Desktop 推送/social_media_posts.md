After 5 years in a Cognitive Neuroscience PhD program, I noticed a pattern: I was reading hundreds of papers but struggling to build a coherent map of the field.

Reading → Notes → Synthesis → Science

Most tools handle step 1. None help with step 3.

So I built PaperHacking Workbench — a desktop app for macOS that turns paper reading into structured knowledge:

📄 Structured templates for Original and Review papers (The Claim, The Gap, Steal This, Schema Update...)

## Product Hunt Post

**Tagline:** A workbench for researchers who want to turn reading into science

**Description:**
```
PaperHacking Workbench is a desktop app for academic researchers who read a lot of papers and want to do more with them.

Instead of scattered notes and forgotten PDFs, you get:

• Structured note templates (Original + Review paper formats)
• Synthesis Mode — auto-extract claims, detect contradictions, map debates across papers
• Personal glossary that auto-links terms to source papers
• Priority-based scheduling (star ratings → weekly reading plans)
• Gamification with streaks and milestones
• Folder-based storage — your data stays on your Mac

Built with Tauri (~3MB, native macOS performance). Free. No account.
```
